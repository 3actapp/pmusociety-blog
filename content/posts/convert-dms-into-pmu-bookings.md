## The Need for Speed: Why Quick Responses Win Bookings

In the world of instant gratification, speed is everything. When a potential client slides into your DMs, they are actively thinking about your services at that very moment. Their interest is at its peak. If you leave them waiting for hours, or even days, that initial excitement will inevitably fade. They will either find another artist who is more responsive, or they will simply lose interest altogether. Responding quickly shows that you are professional, organised, and that you value their time. It immediately sets a positive tone for your interaction and makes the client feel like a priority.

We understand that you are a busy artist. You are likely juggling appointments, consultations, and the hundred other tasks that come with running a business. Replying to every message instantly might seem impossible. However, you do not need to be glued to your phone twenty four seven. The key is to have a system in place. This could involve setting aside specific times throughout the day to check and respond to messages. Even a quick reply acknowledging their message and letting them know when you will be able to provide a more detailed response is better than silence. Remember, a prompt response does not just answer a question. It builds trust and shows that you are a professional who is serious about their business.

## Automate to Elevate: Using Auto Replies and Lead Forms

This brings us to our next point, automation. If you are serious about scaling your PMU business, you cannot afford to manually handle every single initial enquiry. This is where tools like auto replies and lead forms become your best friends. Our own CRM, Scale My Salon, which is a specialised version of GoHighLevel, has these features built in for this very reason. An automated reply can be set up to instantly respond to any new message. This message can thank the person for their interest, provide some basic information, and most importantly, direct them to the next step in your booking process.

This next step should ideally be a lead capture form. Instead of engaging in a lengthy back and forth conversation in your DMs, you can direct potential clients to a simple form on your website. This form can ask for all the necessary information you need to qualify them as a lead, such as their name, contact details, the treatment they are interested in, and any relevant medical history. This not only saves you a huge amount of time, but it also organises all your leads in one central place. It professionalises your process and moves the conversation from a casual chat to a formal booking enquiry. This simple shift in your process can have a massive impact on your conversion rate.

## Show Me the Money: Making Deposit Information Clear

One of the biggest hurdles in securing a booking is the deposit. Many artists feel awkward bringing up money too early in the conversation, but this is a mistake. Being upfront about your deposit requirements is crucial for filtering out time wasters and securing serious clients. Your deposit is a commitment from the client that they are serious about the appointment. It protects you from no shows and last minute cancellations, which can be devastating for a small business. Do not hide your deposit information in the small print. Make it a clear and non negotiable part of your booking process.

Your automated reply or the information on your lead form page should clearly state your deposit policy. This includes the amount, the payment methods you accept, and your cancellation or rescheduling policy. By being transparent from the outset, you are setting clear expectations and avoiding any awkward conversations later on. A client who is serious about booking with you will have no problem paying a deposit. In fact, it often makes them feel more secure in their booking. It shows that you are a professional business with clear policies in place. So, do not be shy. Be confident in your value and make your deposit information front and centre.

## Your Digital Storefront: The Power of a Professional Website

Your social media profile might be where clients discover you, but your website is where they are converted. A professional, easy to navigate website is absolutely essential for any serious PMU artist. It is your digital storefront, a place where you can showcase your work, build trust, and provide all the information a potential client needs to make a booking decision. If you are relying solely on your Instagram or Facebook page, you are leaving money on the table. A website gives you a level of control and professionalism that you simply cannot achieve on social media alone.

One of the most important pages on your website is your Frequently Asked Questions or FAQ page. This is your opportunity to proactively address any questions or concerns a potential client might have. Think about all the common questions you get in your DMs. What is the healing process like? Does it hurt? How long does it last? Am I a suitable candidate? By answering these questions in detail on your website, you are not only saving yourself time, but you are also demonstrating your expertise and building trust with potential clients. When a client can find all the information they need easily, they are much more likely to feel confident in their decision to book with you.

## The Fortune is in the Follow Up: How to Follow Up Without Being Pushy

So, you have responded quickly, directed them to a lead form, and they have all the information they need. But what if they still have not booked? This is where the follow up comes in. So many artists miss out on bookings simply because they fail to follow up with potential clients. People are busy, and they get distracted. A polite follow up can be all it takes to bring your services back to the top of their mind and nudge them towards making a booking.

We recommend a simple two step follow up process. A day or two after they have submitted a lead form, send them a polite message. You can say something like, “Hi [Name], just wanted to follow up on the enquiry you sent over. Do you have any questions I can help with before you are ready to book your appointment?”. If you still do not hear back, wait another few days and send one final, friendly reminder. This shows that you are attentive and eager for their business, without being pushy or desperate. It is a simple, yet incredibly effective way to increase your booking rate. Remember, the fortune is in the follow up.
