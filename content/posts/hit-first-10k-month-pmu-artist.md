## The Ten Thousand Pound Question: Breaking Down the Maths

Let’s get straight to the point. Ten thousand pounds in a month sounds like a lot of money. And it is. But let’s demystify it. It is not some mythical number pulled from thin air. It is a series of smaller, achievable steps that, when followed consistently, lead to a predictable outcome. The problem is that most artists never do the maths. They see the big number and immediately feel overwhelmed.

Let’s say your average treatment price is £350. To hit £10,000, you need to secure just under 29 bookings in a month. That is approximately seven clients a week. Suddenly, it does not seem so impossible, does it? When you look at it like that, the goal becomes tangible. It becomes real. It moves from a 'what if' to a 'how to'.

Your pricing strategy is a critical lever here. Consider this simple breakdown:

| Average Price Per Treatment | Bookings Needed for £10k | Average Bookings Per Week |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| £250 | 40 | 10 |
| £350 | 29 | 7 |
| £450 | 23 | 6 |

As you can see, increasing your price not only reflects the quality of your work but also reduces the number of clients you need to see. This is not about working harder; it is about working smarter. Of course, we are not saying it is easy. It requires work. But it is simple. It is a numbers game. And it is a game you can win. You just need to know the rules and have a system to execute the plan.
